## Full description

Email Signature Studio is a Windows/Mac/Linux desktop app that replaces email-signature SaaS subscriptions with a $15 one-time purchase.

**What it does**
- 10 hand-built templates (Classic, Modern Sidebar, Minimal, Bold Accent Bar, Card, Compact, Corporate, Creative, Photo Left, Photo Top)
- Email-safe output: table layouts, inline styles only, Outlook VML conditional comments for buttons — renders correctly in Gmail, Outlook desktop/web, Apple Mail, Thunderbird
- Headshot/logo upload with auto-resize, or hosted-URL mode
- 12 social networks, brand-colored or monochrome
- Optional banner + CTA button, legal disclaimer block
- Live preview with desktop/mobile widths and light/dark backgrounds
- One-click rich copy → paste straight into Gmail's signature box
- In-app install guides for every major client

**Built for teams and agencies**
Import a CSV of your people (or your client's people), pick a template, and batch-export a folder of ready-to-install .html signatures. Signature rollout for a 40-person client becomes a 5-minute job — and you're not paying $6/mo × 40 seats.

**Honest software**
100% local. No account, no telemetry, no vendor branding injected into your signatures. Source is MIT on GitHub; the paid version is the convenience installer.

## Maker first comment

Hey PH 👋

I got tired of paying $6/month — per user! — for what is fundamentally a block of table-based HTML. And the free tiers of these tools quietly advertise *their* brand at the bottom of *your* emails, which always felt backwards.

So I built Email Signature Studio: a desktop app that does the job once, properly. The hard part of email signatures isn't the form — it's the HTML. Every template here is hand-built with tables, inline styles, and Outlook conditional comments, then smoke-tested so nothing ships with external CSS that email clients strip.

The feature I'm most proud of is team mode: drop in a CSV of names/emails/phones, pick a template, and export a folder of ready-to-install signatures. If you're an agency doing signature rollouts for clients, that's the whole pitch.

It's $15 once. The source is MIT on GitHub if you'd rather build it yourself — the paid version is just the 1-click installer plus supporting the project. Happy to answer anything about email-client rendering quirks, of which I now know far too many.
